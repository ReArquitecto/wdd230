Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

netflix.com

Visual hierarchy is the way elements on a web page are arranged in order of importance. By using visual cues such as size, color, and placement, designers can guide users' attention to the most important elements on the page. This can improve the usability and accessibility of a website, making it easier for users to find the information they need and complete tasks.

Hick's Law

pianomarvel.com

Hick's Law states that as the number of options increases, the time it takes to make a decision increases. By minimizing the number of options and simplifying the interface, user's decision-making process. Overwhelming users with too many options may contribute to users leaving the website in search of alternatives.

Fitt's Law

About Safari

Fitt's Law states that the time it takes for a user to move their cursor to a specific target on a screen is directly proportional to the distance of the target and the size of the target. It's very important to keep in mind how close different functions of an app are in realation to each other. We also don't want mobile users to have to reach to the top of the screen.
